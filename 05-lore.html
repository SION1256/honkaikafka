CHAPTER V  ·  CHARACTER LORE

Nihility's Servant

✦

From the dying world of Petruges-V — a planet infected by a Stellaron where the very capacity for fear had been erased from its inhabitants — there emerged a woman who would come to embody something far more dangerous than fear itself. Kafka.

Born into a world where people know not the meaning of terror, Kafka became a Devil Hunter, pursuing the twisted pleasure-driven souls the Stellaron's influence had created. But dissatisfaction gnawed at her. The status quo offered no answers to the question she could never silence: what is the true value of life?

"Kafka knows not what fear is, yet she excels at instilling the deepest fear in her enemies."
— INTERASTRAL PEACE CORPORATION INTELLIGENCE BRIEF

She found her answer — or perhaps only deeper questions — in Elio, the Destiny's Slave, whose script weaves futures with the precision of a loom. As one of Elio's most trusted agents, Kafka follows the script not out of blind devotion, but because it leads toward something she cannot yet name.

Her true weapon is language. Spirit Whisper works because she speaks the words people already want to hear. She doesn't manipulate so much as she reveals — like a spider who doesn't chase its prey but simply waits in a perfect web of understanding. Few have ever escaped.

The Trailblazer — the vessel she chose, the being she implanted with a Stellaron — was not merely a mission. She taught them. Protected them. Chose their name. Whether this is part of Elio's script or something entirely her own remains, like Kafka herself, beautifully and deliberately unclear.
